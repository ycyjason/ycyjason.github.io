Project Vision

Four Seasons Hotels Limited, trading as Four Seasons Hotels and Resorts, is an international luxury hotel and resort company. For this project, I decided to redesign the current mobile app that they launched for 7 years. The new product aimed to create a delightful user experience for booking and managing users’ reservations.

Problems

  1. The reservation process is complicated and frustrating for users
  2. Users cannot manage their reservations
  3. The conversion rate is low due to the lack of app features
  4. Users get confused when they cannot contact resort staff for enquiries

Understanding The Users

Starting off, I began with gathering user feedback on the current app. The purpose is to understand how users feel about the app and what problems they had encountered with the app.

Key User Problems

  • Information are not well listed on the app
  • Users cannot manage their reservation
  • The conversion rate is low due to the lack of app features
  • Users get lost about the reservation status

User Interviews

I conducted 4 informal interviews with my colleagues at work and friends. The goal is to identify the common challenges of people reserving and managing their booking online. Besides, it is also important to understand what bring a seamless booking experience for them.

Research Questions

  • Can you describe the process when you make a reservation for a hotel room?
  • What challenges did you face when browsing/reserving a room?
  • What will you do during your stay in a hotel?
  • Tell me about last time you used a booking app?

Major Insights

  • Interviewees prefer browsing the accommodations with detailed information, e.g. room photos, facilities, bed types, etc.
  • Interviewees prioritise the price of the accommodations.
  • Interviewees tend to reserve accommodation with a package including, e.g. breakfast included.

Personas

After conducting the user interviews, I created two personas based on the common user goals and pain points.

Sketches

When I started sketching the paper wireframes, I also thought of the information architecture of the app. During the brainstorming and sketching process, I had to keep in mind that every page was created with its purpose, not making them fancy but useless. It was relatively difficult to create a minimalistic UI while keeping products as the focus. I made a different version of the wireframes by trying out various layouts, and removing redundant elements.

Usability Study

I transformed my sketches into low-fidelity wireframes and prototypes. The goal was to test out the usability of my design. I conducted a usability study with 5 participants, ages 20 to 55. After collecting their feedbacks, I then segmented the data into different themes with an affinity diagram. As a result, more insights can be generated to make further improvement.

Major Insights

  • Users want the payment to be more accessible and easy to follow
  • Users want a booking confirmation page to double check the booking details
  • Users want to have more control over the search result to find out the best accommodation for them
  • Users prefer to fill in the check in and out date by using a calendar

Testing & Iterations

Based on the insights generated from the affinity diagram, I iterated my design and continually tested the functionality. Thus, 4 major improvements have been made.

1. Better Payment Experiences

  • Payment method and details insert can be accessible within a single page
  • Avoid users get lost when they are jumped to next page
  • Provide more confidence to the users for making payment

2. Booking Confirmation Page

  • Users can double check to confirm their booking
  • Act as a receipt for users
  • Allows a better view of their booking confirmation

3. Sorting and Filtering

  • Users can have more control over the search result
  • Allows more effective searching

4. Advance Information Entering Method

  • Allows users select the dates from long future
  • Better way to display and entering the information

Takeaways

I am so glad that I have worked on this UX Design study. I gained a lot of insights into conducting different UX research. For me, understanding the true users’ needs was the most challenging part. To be a professional UX designer, it is so important to keep in mind putting the user front and centre. We are not creating the product for our own, instead, we are creating the product for “users”. The most exciting and rewarding part of the project was conducting usability studies. I made so many assumptions about my design but turned out, that was not what users wanted. The study allows me to understand the users more, and the only thing I should do is to be open-minded. I heard a quote, “UX designer should aim to create the product to be useful, instead of useless but pretty”, and I cannot agree more with that.
