Project Vision

Fillfull is an e-commerce platform that specialises in snack retailing, initiated by A.S. Watson Group, and built with Shopify with Google Analytics installed. The platform aims to provide busy professionals, families, and health-conscious individuals in the Hong Kong market with a convenient and enjoyable shopping experience by offering a wide range of snacks.

Tasks

  • Generated UX insights from the data of Google Analytics
  • Designed various campaign websites
  • Developed website modules and functions to enhance UX

Discover

The UX research in this project involved using Google Analytics to gain interesting and valuable insights into how users interact with the website. To examine the user journey of the website and identify the potential area for the improvement in UX, data have been tracked and analysed in the process.

Data That Have Been Used

  • Conversion rate
  • Site loading speed
  • Bounce rate
  • Acquisition device

Major Problems

  • The conversion rates did not achieve as expect for some new campaigns
  • Long loading time for the website
  • Some pages, such as cart page and product details page, had unexpected high dropout rate
  • The website did not function properly in mobile device

Explore

The problems and insights were reported to the marketing and design teams to brainstorm different ideas and solutions for specific issues. During this process, we were able to provide valid solutions based on others' experiences and knowledge.

Possible UX Enhancements

1. Improve site speed by optimising the website images

Since the design team did not have a guideline for designing images for the website, most of the images' sizes were larger than 5MB and their dimensions were not consistent.

2. Optimise for the device used by the majority of users

According to the data from Google Analytics, the majority of users access the website using their mobile devices. However, not all pages were designed to be responsive.

3. Increase the conversion rate with personalised content

It was discovered that the appearance of the newly launched campaign was lacking, causing users not to notice that new products were on sale.

4. Modify the pages with unexpected high bounce rate

Some of the pages did not provide breadcrumb navigation or a return button, causing users to drop out instead of being able to return to the previous page when they make a mistake.

Build and Test

To improve the consistency and usability of the website, a process was undertaken to develop new functions and create design guidelines. Once the development phase was complete, the new functions and design changes were implemented into the backend server for testing. The testing process involved identifying and addressing any issues or bugs in the modules before they were launched on the live website. As a result, 4 major improvements were made to the website.

1. Developed Design Guideline

Documentation has been prepared for the design team, providing guidance on image dimensions for different devices and content types. The documentation also specifies that image sizes should be less than 1MB to improve loading speed.

2. Created Responsive Website

A responsive website was created to ensure compatibility across desktop, tablet, and mobile devices. The content, images, and layout were adjusted to fit the screen size of the device being used by the user.

3. Developed Product Recommendation Modules

The cart page was enhanced by suggesting promotional products to users, and a floating banner was also created to encourage them to take advantage of the discounts available.

4. Provided Return Pathway

It allows users to easily navigate back to previous pages or steps in their shopping journey, which can help them avoid getting lost or frustrated.

Result

After implementing the solutions, I tracked the data from Google Analytics for a month to evaluate the performance of each solution. The results obtained reflected significant improvements in four key areas.

Major Improvements

  • Average page load time dropped from 4.8s to 1.8s
  • Received positive feedback from users through a survey conducted by the marketing team
  • Increased number to the newly launched campaign page
  • Some pages have a significantly reduced bounce rate

Takeaways

In this project, it was evident that qualitative research combined with a large amount of data provided valuable insights for enhancing the website's UX. Effective collaboration with the design and marketing team was also crucial, as insights gathered from users were shared to help them better understand their target audience and provide more user-centred services. Overall, the project demonstrated the importance of combining various research methods and cross-team collaboration to improve the user experience of a website.
