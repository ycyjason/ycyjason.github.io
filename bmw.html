Role

  • Prototyper

Duration

  • Sep 2022 (6 months)

Project Vision

In this project, a mobile app has been created for BMW(HK) to promote customer loyalty and retention through a personalized rewards program and exclusive privileges. The app features a clear and user-friendly interface, with easily accessible relevant information and engaging gamification elements. The aim is to build a loyal user base and enhance the overall experience of owning a BMW.

Tasks

  • Designed the UIs of the app to meet both the branding vision and the client's expectations
  • Conducted research to identify potential UX enhancements
  • Worked closely with the developers to ensure the design is implemented correctly

Discover

This process involved collaboration with the data analytics team. In previous projects, similar loyalty app mechanisms were designed and launched with integrated data trackers. As a result, interesting UX insights were gained through analysis of the data.

Potential Users Problems

  • Users lacked incentives to upgrade their tier
  • Users did not feel motivated to progress in their tier
  • The UIs for displaying mission and point rewards were not compelling

Causes

  • Lack of personalised information displayed at the right time
  • The privileges for different tiers were not adequately displayed or communicated to users
  • The progress bar did not clearly indicate the user's progress towards rewards or upgrading tiers

Explore

We, design team, conducted a design sprint session during the exploration phase to brainstorm potential solutions for the identified UX problems. As a result, we concluded on the most promising UX enhancements which could be implement in the design to address these issues.

Possible UX Enhancements

  • Implement notification bubbles and popup messages in the app
  • Design a better representation for the progress bar
  • Implement tooltips in the app

Build and Test

I created multiple versions of the prototype to test and validate the design concepts. During this process, I also collaborated with the development team to receive feedback and ensure the feasibility of implementing the design concepts. This step was essential in refining the design and ensuring that it met both the user's needs and technical constraints. As a result, we were able to finalise three design solutions that addressed the identified issues.

1. Notification Bubbles

It can help improve user engagement and keep them up-to-date with promotions as well as progress.

2. Better Representation For Progress Bar

New progress bar’s design has a clear visual indication of the user's progress towards their goal.

3. Tooltips

It can help users understand the app better and navigate it more effectively.

Takeaways

The main takeaway from this project is that a thorough understanding of the user's needs and pain points is critical in designing an effective and user-friendly product. Through the design sprint and prototyping process, the team was able to identify and address several UX issues in the app, including a lack of incentives for users to upgrade their tier, a confusing progress bar, and a lack of information about the privileges for different tiers. Besides, it is a good practice to involve the developer in the early design stage, so to ensure the final product meets both user needs and technical feasibility.
