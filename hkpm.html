Project Vision

The Hong Kong Palace Museum is a museum located in Hong Kong that showcases a collection of artifacts from the Palace Museum in Beijing. As part of a team of four, we examined how to promote Chinese culture both within and beyond the Hong Kong Palace Museum (HKPM) in order to increase its recognition within the wider Hong Kong community.

Problems

  1. Learning Chinese art and culture is perceived as bland
  2. The need for innovative approaches
  3. Improving customer experiences

Emphasise

To gain a deeper understanding of our user group for the HKPM project, we took several steps. Firstly, we collected user data and conducted interviews with mentors who had experience working on similar projects. Additionally, we conducted extensive research on different museums and their approaches to improving the customer experience and creating engaging content. By doing so, we aimed to gain valuable insights and inspiration that could help us create innovative approaches for HKPM.

Key User Problems

  • The exhibits and displays static are lacking in interactivity
  • Limited interest in Chinese art and culture
  • Crowding and long wait times makes the museum experience less enjoyable

Define

Once we had obtained the necessary background information about the situation and gained an understanding of the user's needs and goals, we proceeded to define the direction for generating possible solutions. This involved conducting research and identifying our target audience. By taking these steps, we aimed to ensure that our solutions were well-informed, relevant, and effective in meeting the needs of our users.

Research Questions

  • What motivates you to visit museums, and what are your expectations for a positive museum experience?
  • How do you prefer to learn about new subjects or topics, and what types of content do you find most engaging and memorable?
  • What are some innovative approaches to museum experiences that would capture your attention?

Major Insights

  • Interviewees feel that the exhibits and displays could provide more information, but they also want them to be presented in a way that is engaging, interactive, visually attractive, and immersive.
  • Interviewees need a way to visit the museum that is convenient, easy to navigate, and accessible.
  • Interviewees have shown interest in participating in social and cultural events organised by the museum, such as workshops, lectures, and performances.

Ideation

After we defined our target audience and identified their problems and needs, we engaged in several rounds of ideation to generate numerous ideas. During these sessions, we brainstormed ideas based on others' thoughts and sought feedback from our mentors.

Possible design concept

  • Display exhibits and content in a fun and engaging way through interactive approaches.
  • Organise social events to enhance community engagement.
  • Provide tailored recommendations for exhibits or products based on users' interests and preferences.

Design

Based on the concept that was defined in the previous process, we decided to present our ideas through a mobile application. Therefore, I developed an information architecture and design system for the app that incorporated HKPM's branding. Once the documentation was ready, I designed the user interfaces and prototyped the user flows to showcase the main features of the app.

Prototype

Once the documentation was ready, I designed the user interfaces and prototyped the user flows to showcase the main features of the app. There are a total of four features that have been designed to meet the users’ needs.

1. Virtual Exhibition

To cater to the Hong Kong community with different levels of understanding, this feature uses AR technology to interact with the exhibitions.

2. Virtual Gallery

AR projection of the porcelain exhibits allows customers to see the gallery outside of the museum.

3. AR Game

Users can collect some treasures inside the museum and convert them into coupons and gifts.

4. Customer Loyalty Program

The customer loyalty program for the HKPM app is designed to incentivise users to engage with the museum and its exhibits.

Takeaways

In this project, our team gathered user data and conducted interviews to identify the users' problems and needs, which helped us define the direction and generate possible solutions. It is important to empathise with the target audience for a successful product. Moreover, since there are no right or wrong answers, we could think outside the box and generate numerous creative ideas throughout the ideation process. Lastly, effective communication and collaboration within the team and with stakeholders are essential to bring success to this project. Every constructive feedback that we received helped us to improve our solutions and design a better user-centered product.
