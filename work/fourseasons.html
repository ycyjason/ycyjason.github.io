Four Seasons Hotel: Reservation & Loyalty App

Goolge UX Certificate | Product Designer, 2022

The Four Seasons Hotel app was redesigned to address long-standing user pain points and enhance the overall experience. This redesign focuses on streamlining the reservation process and improving customer retention by integrating a loyalty rewards program to boost engagement and conversions.


While studying Google’s UX course, I learned about industry design conventions and various methodologies. To reinforce my learning, I set myself a challenge: conducting a full-scale case study to apply my knowledge in a real-world scenario. Through this project, I went through the entire design cycle, from user research (empathising with and analysing user behaviour) to UI design (wire-framing and prototyping), followed by testing and iteration. This hands-on approach deepened my understanding of user-centric design and strengthened my ability to craft solutions that align with user needs.

The challenge

Since its launch nine years ago, the Four Seasons Hotel app has lacked updates based on user feedback, resulting in several usability challenges. The reservation process is overly complicated, making it frustrating for users to book seamlessly. Additionally, the lack of a reservation management feature creates inconvenience, preventing guests from modifying or tracking their bookings easily. With limited essential features, the app struggles to drive conversions, while the absence of direct communication channels leaves users feeling disconnected from resort staff. These pain points highlight the need for a comprehensive redesign to enhance usability, improve engagement, and streamline the booking experience.

Understanding users' needs through different approaches

To ensure a user-centric design, I combined user interviews and persona development to uncover key pain points and expectations. These insights informed design opportunities and guided the creation of a more seamless and intuitive booking experience.

User interviews - insights from four participants on hotel booking experiences

Research questions

•

Can you describe the process when you make a reservation for a hotel room?

•

What challenges did you face when browsing/reserving a room?

•

What will you do during your stay in a hotel?

•

Tell me about last time you used a booking app?

Insights

•

Interviewees prefer browsing the accommodations with detailed information, e.g. room photos, facilities, bed types, etc.

•

Interviewees prioritise the price of the accommodations.

•

Interviewees tend to reserve accommodation with a package including, e.g. breakfast included.

Prototype testing & iteration

To inform my design decisions, I developed low-fidelity prototypes from initial sketches and conducted usability tests with five participants aged 20 to 55. Based on the feedback, I analysed the data and identified key areas for improvement, which led to updates such as a streamlined payment module, an enhanced booking confirmation page, and added filtering and sorting features for a more intuitive user experience.

Insights

•

Users want easier and more accessible payment

•

Users want a booking confirmation page for verification

•

Users want more control over search

•

Users prefer using a calendar for dates

Changes made

•

Redesigned payment experience to a module, making payment details accessible on a single page.

•

Enhanced the booking confirmation page, allowing users to confirm details more easily.

•

Added sorting and filtering features for better control and more effective searching.

•

Introduced a calendar interface for selecting check-in and check-out dates for better usability.

Redesigning to address existing user problems

The final app features have been designed with a high-fidelity prototype, aligning closely with the Four Seasons branding. The new design effectively addresses existing user problems, providing an enhanced and intuitive experience.

Optimising search & relevant information display

User pain point

The original design lacked an efficient way for users to search for accommodations based on key criteria like date, location, and group size. Instead, users had to manually browse through different locations in a gallery, making it difficult to find suitable options quickly. This resulted in a time-consuming and frustrating experience.

Goal

Users need a more effective and straightforward way to search for accommodations based on their specific requirements. Additionally, they should be able to view relevant and useful information while keeping the search functionality easily accessible.

Design solution

✅

Redesigned the landing page to display relevant and useful information.

✅

Integrated an easily accessible search feature for quick and efficient accommodation discovery.

✅

Streamlined the search process to enhance usability and reduce friction.

Streamlining booking management for a hassle-free experience

User pain point

The original design allows users to check in via the app but lacks essential details like booking information, pricing, and the hotel’s exact location. Users also cannot modify or cancel their bookings, often leading to frustrating and unsuccessful attempts to contact customer service.

Goal

Users need a seamless way to track, manage, and modify their bookings. They also want the ability to share reservation details easily.

Design solution

✅

Introduced a centralised Reservation History page for quick access to all bookings.

✅

Enabled users to track upcoming stays, view booking history, and access important trip details in one place.

✅

Added options to modify or cancel bookings, reducing reliance on customer service.

Boosting engagement through exclusive rewards

User pain point

Low user retention and engagement due to a lack of incentives for repeat bookings and the absence of attractive offers.

Goal

Encourage loyalty by offering exclusive perks and rewards to returning users, enhancing their overall experience and engagement.

Design solution

✅

Introduced a loyalty program where users earn points based on their bookings and transactions at Four Seasons hotels.

✅

Designed a Redemption Page where users can redeem rewards, such as discounts, room upgrades, or special offers, using their accumulated points.

Takeaway

This project helped me apply the knowledge from the Google UX course to a real-world scenario, reinforcing key lessons on user-centric design. Initially, I believed my design would meet users' needs, but usability testing revealed minor issues that I hadn't anticipated. This taught me that user experience is personal and subjective, and we must avoid biases in our designs. Understanding users' true needs was challenging, but it highlighted the importance of being open-minded and putting the user at the centre of the design process. Conducting usability studies was the most rewarding part, showing me that effective design priorities functionality and usability over aesthetics.
